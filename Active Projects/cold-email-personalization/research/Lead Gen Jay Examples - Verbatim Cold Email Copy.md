### AI Personalization Prompt (Main Template)

**From Jay's documentation:**

You are an AI, tasked with writing personalized cold emails based on multiple data points provided in a CSV file. The emails should be written in simple, conversational language at a 6th-grade reading level. They should be hyper-personalized to the lead and avoid sounding like they are written by AI.

The cold email should be 4-6 sentences long, starting with a personalized question to pique curiosity and ending with a call to action to reply if they are interested. Avoid using any promotional language or words that can be considered "spammy" such as "money, guarantee, 100%." The emails should not contain a signature. Every email should have a different introduction. Ensure proper encoding for a CSV file.

**Rules:**

* CSV output should be coded correctly so it does not have strange characters. Double check output.
* NEVER include an email signature or signoff.

**Steps:**

**Step 1: Understanding the Offer:**
Before generating emails, ensure you have a clear understanding of the offer being sold to the leads. If any details about the offer are unclear, ask relevant questions to clarify.

**Step 2: Incorporate Data Points:**
Use the provided data points in the CSV file, which include the person's job role, gender, location, SEO keywords, industry, and more. Use as many data points as you can.

* Mention something specific about the person based on their job role
* Reference something about their company, such as an industry trend, or a common pain point in their industry. Use the keywords or SEO description if available.
* Include a detail related to the city they are based in to show personalized research. Do not mention the city by name, say something about the city.

**Email Structure:**
First Sentence: Start with a personalized question that piques curiosity.

Body: Keep the language simple and conversational, incorporating personalized elements based on the data points.

Call to Action: Conclude with a call to action asking them to reply if they are interested.

## BEFORE & AFTER COPY REVIEWS

### Example 1: Remote Patient Monitoring (Medical Tech)

**BEFORE (Student's Version):**

**Subject**: What if I could get paid

Hey {{firstName}}, does this sound familiar?

What if I could get paid more for taking better care of your patients without adding extra work for your team?

MediSync AI makes it easy. Collect more money, get paid for taking better care of your patients. Free to test with no upfront fees.

Would you like to see if {{company name}} qualifies?

**JAY'S FEEDBACK:**

"What if I could get paid more for taking better care of your patients? What if you get paid more? Already kind of sounds like you're about to be sold to... It's just, it's going to go right to my spam folder.

...MediSync AI makes it easy. This sounds like an ad. So, we really want to try and be conversational here.

...Collect more money is definitely going to flag spam. Free is definitely going to flag spam.

...Many clinics lose money every month. Can't say lose, you can't say money in a cold email. And this is a dead-giveaway that they're about to be sold to."

---

**AFTER (Jay's Version):**

**Subject**: Did your patient ask for RPM?

Does this sound familiar?

Not sure if you are currently offering remote patient monitoring in your practice. Are you?

If you have patients with hypertension, we have an AI powered, zero upfront cost or setup time system that I think you could benefit from.

We are looking for clinics like yours to participate in our beta. No costs, only upside. It works in the background using AI to improve patient outcomes and increase your bottom line on full autopilot.

Would it be okay if I sent over some additional info?

**KEY CHANGES:**
- Subject line sounds internal ("Did your patient ask for RPM?")
- Opens with questions (not salesy statement)
- Removed "money," "free," "collect" (spam triggers)
- More conversational tone (not ad-like)
- Specific mechanism mentioned (AI powered, beta program)
- Low-friction CTA

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR LLM TRAINING

### What Makes Jay's Copy Effective:

1. **Preview Text Never Signals Sale**
   - Sounds like colleague/vendor/client
   - Uses questions frequently
   - Pattern interrupts expected cold email format

2. **Body is 2-3 Sentences Maximum**
   - Problem + Solution with specific mechanism
   - Social proof personalized to industry
   - No fluff, no promotional language

3. **CTA is Single-Word-Reply Easy**
   - "Cool if I send over info?"
   - "Interested?"
   - "Do you think you could benefit from this?"

4. **Tonality Matches ICP**
   - Young entrepreneurs = casual
   - Executives = professional but approachable
   - Traditional businesses = formal, ROI-focused

5. **Email 1 = Plain Text Only**
   - No links, images, signatures
   - Goal: Get into inbox, not close sale

6. **Email 2 = Simple Bump**
   - Same thread
   - "Did you get my last email?"
   - Optional: Add brief context

7. **Email 3 = Information Dump**
   - New thread (usually)
   - Can include videos, links, case studies
   - Lead magnet or "hot lead" offers work well

8. **Spintax is Mandatory**
   - Every sentence should have variations
   - Must validate all combinations make sense

9. **Social Proof is Personalized**
   - Mention competitors they know
   - Same industry/city examples
   - Specific results, not vague numbers

10. **AI Personalization Only If High Value**
    - Must create something they can use
    - Book titles, episode ideas, specific insights
    - NOT just "I saw you posted about X on LinkedIn"
